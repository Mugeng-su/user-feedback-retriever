## Summary

This review round identified both product-UX issues and pipeline issues.

Important clarification added after reviewing the successful Gemini result:

1. The later record `cmq86cq6r0003lo4se7y4s9in` was generated by `gemini`.
2. The seven UX/content feedback items still stand even when the underlying model output is real.
3. That means the next iteration should focus less on “did the model run” and more on:
   - output information architecture
   - section purpose clarity
   - evidence framing
   - actionability of recommendations

Pipeline issues discovered during diagnosis:

1. The analyzed record `cmq84qre80000qq58vubs1xhk` was generated by the mock analyzer rather than a real model provider.
2. The comment source included non-comment noise such as blog links, product links, and related-video links.
3. Subtitle `.srt` metadata such as timestamps and numeric blocks were not stripped before analysis.
4. Real-provider failures were previously able to fall back silently to the mock analyzer, which made result quality harder to debug.

Fixes shipped in v0.2:

1. Added transcript and comment preprocessing before model analysis.
2. Added provider tracking via `providerUsed`.
3. Changed fallback behavior so failed real providers no longer silently degrade into mock output after attempted execution.
4. Updated prompt rules so explanatory sections follow UI language, while selected phrase-library fields preserve source-language expressions.

## Review Feedback

### 1. Why It Worked section needs categorization

- Problem:
  - The current content is presented as a flat list, which feels unstructured and hard to scan.
- Suggested direction:
  - Group the section into clear buckets such as:
    - content hook
    - trust/proof
    - audience resonance
    - non-reusable or contextual factors
- Why this matters:
  - Helps users distinguish reusable drivers from situational luck.

### 2. Next Content Suggestions should focus more on strategic directions than title-like suggestions

- Problem:
  - The current suggestions feel too close to proposed next titles or direct content prompts.
- Suggested direction:
  - Present:
    - possible content directions
    - why each direction is worth testing
    - what signal in transcript/comments supports it
- Why this matters:
  - Makes the output more useful for planning, briefing, and team discussion.

### 3. Video Breakdown should include timestamps and rough duration share

- Problem:
  - The current breakdown names the sections but does not anchor them in time.
- Suggested direction:
  - For each section such as hook/problem/solution/proof/ending:
    - show approximate start-end timestamp
    - show rough percent of total video duration
- Why this matters:
  - Makes the output materially more actionable for content replication.

### 4. Emoji handling in quoted originals needs cleanup

- Problem:
  - Emoji inside original comments may reduce readability or make phrase extraction noisy.
- Suggested direction:
  - Preferred approach:
    - convert emoji into readable labels when they add meaning
  - fallback approach:
    - remove decorative emoji if they do not carry semantic value
- Why this matters:
  - Improves clarity without losing signal.

### 5. Comment Insights section needs a clearer purpose

- Problem:
  - The section appears useful, but its downstream use is not currently obvious.
- Suggested direction:
  - Add framing text to explain this section is for:
    - understanding the dominant discussion themes
    - spotting objections versus praise
    - identifying what deserves follow-up content
- Why this matters:
  - Helps users know when and how to use the section in review meetings.

### 6. Raw Quotes and Benefit Phrases need clearer selection logic and clearer intended use

- Problem:
  - These two sections do not yet clearly communicate:
    - why these lines were selected
    - how they differ from each other
    - how they should be used afterward
- Suggested direction:
  - Raw Quotes:
    - emphasize authentic user phrasing and representative voice samples
  - Benefit Phrases:
    - emphasize compressed, reusable value-language patterns extracted from comments
  - Add guidance on how to use them for:
    - scripts
    - landing pages
    - product copy
    - ad hooks
- Why this matters:
  - This area is central to the original product thesis and must become much clearer.

### 7. SEO Phrases section needs guidance text and possibly a different presentation

- Problem:
  - The section currently feels awkward and under-explained.
- Suggested direction:
  - Add explanatory copy that clarifies these are:
    - search-intent-like phrases inferred from how users ask or compare
  - Consider presenting them with:
    - confidence
    - evidence source
    - possible usage context
- Why this matters:
  - Prevents the section from feeling arbitrary or mechanically generated.
